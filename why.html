What sets us apart?

Our seventh sense guides us to do things a little differently, so we pioneered an innovative business model called the "30-Minute Employee." This means engaging our network of engineers, tech geeks and professional consultants in 30 minute (unpaid) brainstorming sessions. Using FaceTime, a whiteboard and the law of synergy this model allows us to deliver industry leading insights at a fraction of the cost.
You could probably find somebody to help you with your brand, or look at your organizational structure, or clarify your strategic purpose; and were not stopping you from doing that. But if you can find someone that will look at all of these elements and the interconnectivities between them, for the rates that we charge, then we will dissolve right now!

We take a big picture approach to your business’s future by attacking the major business functions from finance to customer service to innovation, and the interconnectivities between these functional areas. At the same time, we break out the microscope and comb through fine details and intricate components of your business to diagnose areas for growth and improvement.
We maximize the upside of working with 19-22 year olds. Our brains are not only full of energy but creative and innovative ideas. Everyone on our team has a background of entrepreneurial success, so when we hit the whiteboards we are able to generate ideas beyond the individual imagination.
By working with students you’re tapping into our network of the future corporate elite. Our peers have not experienced enough success to stick their noses up to us. Instead they are so driven to be successful that they are actually motivated to contribute to our innovative team.

Also, we are taught by a network of those with a proven track record. Our professors not only have decades of experience in the field of business, but they are eager to help their students be the success story to come out of Queen’s University!
We recognize that your resources are valuable. Once we've presented you our home-run solution, we agree on a set of key performance indicators that must be met before we classify the project as a success. Essentially, we won't ask for money until we have created a source of value from our implemented business plan.
